nanokrnl: a Windows kernel, from scratch, in your browser

The end of an era.

An NT-compatible kernel written in Rust, booted by nanox (a ~60 KB x86-64 emulator) and running real, unmodified Windows binaries (cmd.exe, more.com, null.sys) on its own syscalls. Hit Boot and wait for the C:\> prompt.

By Matt Suiche (@msuiche), Fable 5, Opus 4.8. Shout out to Fabrice Bellard.

epilogue.txt

    

      
Both halves were built from scratch for this project: nanokrnl, a Windows-compatible
         operating-system kernel, and nanox, the emulator that boots it in your browser.

      
Many of us spent the last 20 or 30 years on low-level system programming, emulation, reverse
         engineering, and disassembly. Watching a Windows kernel, and the emulator that runs it (both
         written from scratch), boot inside a browser tab feels like full circle: the close of one era
         as another begins.
